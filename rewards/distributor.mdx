## Overview

Every protocol needs to decide how to allocate revenue. Most make this decision once, at launch, and it never changes. Some don't bother at all.

Ponzu's Distributor makes this decision dynamically based on claim progress. As presale participants claim their tokens, the allocation shifts automatically. No governance votes. No admin intervention. No one can change the formula after deployment.

---

## Income Sources

The Distributor receives value from three sources:

| Source | Type | When |
|--------|------|------|
| Unvested tokens from early claimers | Project tokens | During vesting period |
| Refund penalties (10% of tokens) | Project tokens | Before launch |
| PonzuSwap trading fees (1%) | WETH | Ongoing after launch |

LP tokens from initial liquidity are held permanently by the Presale contract. They never enter the Distributor. This is what makes liquidity unruggable: the LP tokens sit in a contract with no withdrawal function.

---

## Token Distribution

When the Distributor receives project tokens (from early claims and refund penalties), it splits them across four recipients:

| Recipient | Initial (0% claimed) | Mid (50% claimed) | Final (100% claimed) |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Presale holders | 40% | 20% | 0% |
| Farm stakers | 40% | 60% | 80% |
| Project treasury | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| Kioke holders | 5% | 5% | 5% |

**The shift formula:**
```
presaleShare = 40% x (1 - totalClaimed / totalClaimableAmount)
farmShare    = 40% + (40% x totalClaimed / totalClaimableAmount)
```

`totalClaimableAmount` is the actual number of tokens sold during presale (accounting for any refund adjustments). As presale participants claim, their allocation decreases and the farm's increases. When the last person claims, the presale share hits 0% and the farm receives 80%.

**Project treasury** = the wallet of the project creator who deployed the token. This is their ongoing revenue share from the project they built.

**Kioke holders** = holders of Kioke Card NFTs who have endorsed this specific project with Koji credits.

---

## ETH Distribution

When the Distributor receives WETH from swap fees, a fifth recipient enters the split: the Ponzu protocol itself.

| Recipient | Initial (0% claimed) | Mid (50% claimed) | Final (100% claimed) |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Ponzu protocol | 40% | 40% | 40% |
| Presale holders | 20% | 10% | 0% |
| Farm stakers | 20% | 30% | 40% |
| Project treasury | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| Kioke holders | 5% | 5% | 5% |

The key difference from token distribution: ETH splits include a fixed 40% protocol fee. The dynamic presale-to-farm shift operates on a smaller range (20% instead of 40%) because the protocol takes its share first.

**ETH shift formula:**
```
presaleShare = 20% x (1 - totalClaimed / totalClaimableAmount)
farmShare    = 20% + (20% x totalClaimed / totalClaimableAmount)
```

## Worked Example

Assume a project generates 10 ETH in swap fees when 50% of presale tokens have been claimed:

| Recipient | Share | Amount |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Ponzu protocol | 40% | 4.0 ETH |
| Presale holders | 10% | 1.0 ETH |
| Farm stakers | 30% | 3.0 ETH |
| Project treasury | 15% | 1.5 ETH |
| Kioke holders | 5% | 0.5 ETH |

If you held 5% of the presale and hadn't claimed your tokens yet, your ETH reward from this batch: 5% of 1.0 ETH = 0.05 ETH. Claimable immediately. Repeatable every time new fees accumulate.

Now assume 5,000 tokens were forfeited by early claimers during that same period:

| Recipient | Share | Amount |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Presale holders | 20% | 1,000 tokens |
| Farm stakers | 60% | 3,000 tokens |
| Project treasury | 15% | 750 tokens |
| Kioke holders | 5% | 250 tokens |

## Why Dynamic Allocation?

The presale allocation provides rewards to holders during the vesting period. But presale participants can only claim tokens once. Continuing to reward an empty presale pool would mean burning value.

Instead, as the presale empties, its allocation shifts to the farm. The farm extends the alignment horizon beyond the initial vesting period. After diamond-hand vesting completes, there's natural sell pressure from holders who waited but are ready to exit. The farm gives a reason to stay: stake LP tokens, earn non-inflationary rewards from fees.

Conviction, extended.

## Lifetime ETH Rewards

Even after the vesting period, Ponzu Bottle holders who haven't claimed their tokens continue earning ETH from the Distributor. ETH rewards are calculated pro-rata based on your share of the presale:

```
yourETHReward = (yourPurchases / totalPurchases) x ethSentToPresale
```

ETH rewards can be claimed at any time, repeatedly, without affecting your token claim. Each time you call `claimETH(tokenId)`, you receive all accumulated ETH since your last claim. There's no minimum threshold.

Once you claim your tokens (burning your Ponzu Bottle), your ETH rewards stop. The tradeoff: claim tokens early and lose future ETH income, or hold your Ponzu Bottle and keep collecting.

## Permissionless Operation

The Distributor has no admin functions. Once initialized at deployment:

- Split ratios are calculated algorithmically from on-chain state
- Anyone can trigger distribution by calling `distribute(token)` or `distributeETH()`
- No one can change the formula
- No one can redirect funds
- The `init()` function can only be called once (reverts with `AlreadyInitialized` on any second call)
- No proxy pattern. No upgrade mechanism. The bytecode is final.

**What about griefing?** If someone calls `distribute()` when only dust amounts are in the contract, the transaction succeeds but distributes negligible value. This costs the caller gas but doesn't affect other users. Tokens accumulate until someone triggers distribution. Your share doesn't change regardless of when distribution is called.

**What about random tokens?** If someone sends a random ERC20 to the Distributor and calls `distribute(randomToken)`, it splits that token by the current ratios. No harm to the core token or ETH flows. WETH sent to `distribute()` is rejected. WETH must go through `distributeETH()`, which unwraps it to native ETH first.

## On-Chain Verification

Every Distributor deploys with view functions you can call directly on Etherscan:

| Function | Returns |
|----------|---------|
| `getCurrentAllocations()` | Current basis point split for all recipients |
| `presaleAllocationBP()` | Current presale share in basis points |
| `farmAllocationBP()` | Current farm share in basis points |
| `getBalance(token)` | Undistributed token balance |
| `getETHBalance()` | Undistributed ETH balance |

No trust required. Read the contract state directly.
