## Why Ponzu

Every Ponzu launch enforces the same rules through immutable contracts. No insider allocations. No hidden OTC deals. No surprise unlocks. No admin keys that can change the rules after you buy in.

The question isn't "will this rug?" The contracts make the classic rug mechanics impossible. The question is whether the project team will build something worth holding.

## Protection Against Rugs

### What the Contracts Prevent

- **No team allocation.** 0% to team, VCs, or treasury at token creation. 69% to presale buyers, 31% to permanent DEX liquidity.
- **Immutable contracts.** No admin keys. No upgrade proxies. No owner functions. The deployed bytecode is final. Verify it on Etherscan yourself.
- **Permanent liquidity.** LP tokens are held by the Launcher contract. There is no `withdraw`, `remove`, or `rescue` function. Not time-locked (which implies an unlock). The withdrawal function does not exist in the code.
- **Refund before launch.** Get 90% of your ETH back before the presale completes. The 10% token penalty redistributes pro-rata to remaining holders.

### What the Contracts Enforce

- **Diamond-hand vesting.** After launch, tokens vest linearly over 10 days (or 10 weeks). Claiming early forfeits unvested tokens to the Distributor, which sends them pro-rata to patient holders and farm stakers.
- **Anti-sniper fees.** 20% swap fee at launch, decaying linearly to 1% over the first hour. Sniping a launch costs 20% of the trade on entry. Economically irrational at any reasonable position size.
- **Fixed supply.** 1,000,000 tokens. No mint function in the contract. No inflation. No dilution. Ever.
- **Transparent fees.** 4% presale fee (1% creator, 1% protocol, 1% Kioke holders, 1% order referrer). 1% swap fee (permanent). All verifiable on-chain.

### What the Contracts Don't Prevent

Be clear-eyed about what remains outside contract scope:

- **Team abandonment.** The team can stop building after launch. Contracts enforce token mechanics, not team effort. Evaluate the team, not just the structure.
- **Price decline.** Permanent liquidity means there's always a pool to sell into, but it doesn't guarantee price. If the project fails to deliver value, the token price reflects that.
- **Low trading volume.** ETH rewards come from swap fees. A project with no trading volume generates no ETH rewards. Rewards scale with activity, not with time held.

## Your Position is an NFT

When you buy into a presale, you receive a **Ponzu Bottle** (ERC-721). This NFT represents your entire position: token allocation, ETH contributed, vesting status, and accumulated ETH rewards.

**Why this matters for large positions:**
- **Liquidity without dumping.** Need to exit a $500K position before claiming? Sell the Ponzu Bottle on secondary markets. The token supply is unaffected. No price impact on the project's DEX pool.
- **Price discovery.** A presale position in a project valued at $400K can be listed on OpenSea. Buyers evaluate the project's trajectory, not just the token's spot price.
- **Full transfer of rights.** New owner inherits the complete position: token claim rights, refund option (if pre-launch), and accumulated ETH rewards.

## The Vesting Decision

After launch, you choose when to claim your tokens. One claim, one time, irreversible.

| Claim Timing | Tokens You Receive | Tokens Forfeited to Community |
|-------------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| Day 1 of 10 | 10% of allocation | 90% |
| Day 3 of 10 | 30% of allocation | 70% |
| Day 5 of 10 | 50% of allocation | 50% |
| Day 10 of 10 | 100% + bonus tokens | 0% |

### Worked Example: $500K Position

Suppose you deploy $500K into a presale that raises $2M total. You hold 25% of the presale (172,500 tokens out of 690,000).

If 30% of other holders claim on Day 1 (forfeiting 90% of their tokens):
- They forfeit approximately 139,725 tokens to the Distributor
- The Distributor routes 40% of those tokens to remaining presale holders pro-rata
- Your share of forfeited tokens: ~18,630 bonus tokens (your 25% of the 40% presale allocation)
- You now hold 172,500 + 18,630 = 191,130 tokens at full vest

Your patience earned you a 10.8% bonus on top of your full allocation. And that's only from the presale holder portion of the Distributor. Farm stakers receive another 40%.

### The Math on Early Claims

If the token trades at 20x your entry price on Day 1, an immediate claim gives you 10% of your tokens at 20x = 2x return. Waiting to Day 10 gives you 100% at whatever the price is then, plus bonus tokens from everyone who couldn't wait. The vesting curve makes patience the default profitable strategy for anyone with a multi-day time horizon.

## ETH Rewards

While vesting, your Ponzu Bottle accumulates ETH from the Distributor. The Distributor collects 1% of every swap on PonzuSwap for that token pair. ETH rewards are claimable repeatedly at any time without affecting your token position.

### How Rewards Scale

ETH rewards are a function of trading volume, not time.

| Daily Trading Volume | Daily Swap Fees (1%) | Annual to Presale Holders (20% of fees) |
|---------------------|---------------------|--------------------------------------|
| $10K | $100 | ~$7,300 |
| $100K | $1,000 | ~$73,000 |
| $1M | $10,000 | ~$730,000 |

Your share is proportional to your position size relative to unclaimed presale holders. As others claim their tokens and exit the presale pool, your percentage of ETH rewards increases.

Even after your vesting period completes, you can leave your tokens unclaimed and continue earning ETH indefinitely. There is no deadline to claim.

## Position Exit Mechanics

For large positions, exit strategy matters as much as entry.

**Before launch (presale active):**
- Refund: 90% of ETH back. 10% token penalty goes to remaining holders.
- Sell Ponzu Bottle on secondary. Full position transfers to buyer.

**After launch (tokens vesting):**
- Sell Ponzu Bottle on secondary markets (full position, vesting included).
- Wait for vesting to complete, claim tokens, sell into PonzuSwap liquidity pool.

**Liquidity depth consideration:** 31% of total token supply (310,000 tokens) plus their ETH-side pair are permanently in the liquidity pool. On a $2M raise, approximately $620K of ETH seeds the pool at launch (31% of raise). Your exit slippage depends on your position size relative to pool depth. A $500K sell against a $620K pool would have significant slippage. A $50K sell would not. For large positions, the Ponzu Bottle secondary market or gradual selling across multiple transactions will typically give better execution.
