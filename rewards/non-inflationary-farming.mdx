## The Problem with Traditional Farming

Inflation-based farming destroyed projects in 2021. Mint new tokens, distribute to stakers, call it "yield." The arithmetic is obvious: you're diluting existing holders to pay new ones. Industrial farming bots made it worse. Constant sell pressure from freshly minted reward tokens. Death by a thousand sells.

---

## How Ponzu Farming Works

Ponzu's farm distributes rewards from actual economic activity. No new tokens are ever minted. The token contract has no mint function. The total supply is 1,000,000 at creation, and it stays at 1,000,000 forever.

**Reward sources:**
| Source | Type | Origin |
|--------|------|--------|
| Forfeited tokens from early presale claimers | Project tokens | Presale holders who claimed before vesting completed |
| 1% PonzuSwap trading fees | WETH | Every swap on PonzuSwap |
| LP penalties from early farm exits | LP tokens | Farmers who unstaked before 7 days |

If trading volume grows, ETH rewards grow. If more people claim early, token rewards grow. The feedback loop is honest: project health directly determines farm yield.

## Staking Mechanics

1. Get LP tokens by providing liquidity on PonzuSwap, or use ZapETH to convert ETH to LP in one transaction
2. Stake LP tokens in the Farm contract
3. Each stake mints a **Liquidity Card NFT** (ERC-721)
4. Rewards accumulate proportionally to your staked LP relative to total staked LP

**No top-ups.** Each `stakeNewCard()` call creates a new Liquidity Card with its own penalty timer and reward tracking. You can't add more LP to an existing card.

### ZapETH

The `zapETHToLP(tokenAddress, minLPTokens)` function converts ETH into LP tokens in a single transaction. Under the hood, it splits your ETH, swaps half for project tokens, and adds both sides as liquidity.

The `minLPTokens` parameter is your slippage protection. Set it to the expected LP output minus your slippage tolerance (e.g., 2%). If front-running or price movement causes the output to fall below your minimum, the transaction reverts and you keep your ETH.

## The 7-Day Conviction Window

The farm has its own conviction mechanism. Unstake before 7 days and you leave a portion of your LP behind.

| Days Staked | Penalty | LP Returned |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| 0 days | 100% | 0% |
| 1 day | 86% | 14% |
| 3 days | 57% | 43% |
| 3.5 days | 50% | 50% |
| 7+ days | 0% | 100% |

**Penalty formula:**
```
penalty = (7 - daysSinceStake) / 7 x 100%
```

At 0 days (same block as staking), the penalty is 100%. You get nothing back. This prevents flash-loan attacks where someone stakes, captures a reward distribution, and immediately unstakes.

**Where does the penalized LP go?**
- 50% added to remaining stakers' reward calculations. Their share of future rewards increases proportionally, as if they had staked more LP. The actual LP tokens stay in the farm contract, backing their position.
- 50% becomes protocol-owned liquidity. Permanently locked. Deepens the trading pool for everyone.

Same principle as diamond-hand vesting: impatient capital subsidizes the patient.

## Claim Types

Rewards earned from the farm come in two forms with different claim rules:

### ETH Rewards (from swap fees via Distributor)
- Claimable anytime via `claimETH(cardId)`
- Repeatable. Claim as often as you want.
- No penalty on ETH claims
- Accumulated ETH is tracked per Liquidity Card

### Token Rewards (from forfeited vesting tokens via Distributor)
- **One-time claim** per Liquidity Card
- Same diamond-hand principle as the presale: claim early and forfeit unvested tokens to remaining farm stakers
- Your vested amount depends on when you claim relative to the reward epoch

**What happens if you unstake before claiming?** Unstaking burns your Liquidity Card. Any unclaimed ETH rewards are claimed automatically during the unstake transaction. Unclaimed token rewards follow the same one-time claim logic: you receive whatever has vested at the time of unstake, and the unvested portion goes to remaining stakers via the Distributor.

## Liquidity Card NFT

Each staking position is represented as an ERC-721 NFT with independent state.

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Minted on | Each `stakeNewCard()` call |
| Per user | Multiple cards allowed |
| Contains | Staked LP amount, stake timestamp, accumulated rewards |
| Transferable | Yes. Position, pending rewards, and penalty timer all transfer with the NFT |
| Burned on | `unstake()` |

**Multi-card strategy:** Stake at different times to manage different penalty clocks. Unstake Card #1 (no penalty after 7 days) while Card #2 continues earning. Claim ETH from any card independently. Each card's rewards are tracked separately.

## Modeling Expected Returns

Farm APY depends on two variables: trading volume and total LP staked. No one can promise a specific return because both change constantly. But here's the formula so you can model it yourself:

**ETH rewards per epoch (7 days):**
```
Your ETH = (yourStakedLP / totalStakedLP) x ethDistributedToFarm
```

Where `ethDistributedToFarm` depends on the Distributor's current farm allocation (20-40% of swap fee ETH, depending on claim progress).

**Example scenario:**
- Weekly swap volume: $500,000
- Swap fee: 1% = $5,000 in fees
- Distributor farm allocation (at 50% claimed): 30%
- ETH to farm: $1,500
- Your LP: 10% of total staked
- Your weekly ETH: $150

That's the model. Volume up = rewards up. More LP staked = your share decreases. No inflation tricks. No vanishing APY from token emissions.

**Token rewards** are harder to predict because they depend on how many presale holders claim early and when. High early-claim activity = more forfeited tokens = more farm rewards.

## Risks to Understand

**Impermanent loss.** When you provide LP, you hold both the project token and ETH. If the token price moves significantly in either direction, your LP position can be worth less than holding the two assets separately. This is standard AMM risk and applies to every LP farm. At large positions ($50K+), impermanent loss can exceed farming rewards during high-volatility periods. Model both sides.

**Low liquidity concentration.** If very little LP is staked in the farm, individual stakers capture disproportionate rewards. This is a feature for early stakers, but it also means large reward batches can be captured by a small number of participants. There's no minimum stake requirement.

**Smart contract risk.** The farm contract is immutable post-deployment. No admin can upgrade, pause, or modify it. This means bugs can't be patched. It also means no one can rug you. Tradeoff accepted.
