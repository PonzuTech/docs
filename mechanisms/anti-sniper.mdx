## Swap Fee Structure

PonzuSwap is a Uniswap V2 fork with two modifications: dynamic launch fees and integrated Distributor routing.

### Normal Trading (After First Hour)

**1% fee on output tokens.** Charged symmetrically:
- Buying (ETH to token): 1% fee deducted from token output
- Selling (token to ETH): 1% fee deducted from ETH output

All fees route to the Distributor, which splits them based on the current allocation formula.

**Note on total trading cost:** The 1% fee is separate from price impact (slippage). On a $10,000 trade, the fee is $100. Price impact depends on pool depth and trade size. Check expected output on PonzuSwap before confirming.

### Launch Window (First Hour)

**20% fee** decaying linearly to **1%** over 60 minutes.

| Time After Launch | Fee Rate |
|-------------------|----------|
| 0 min | 20.0% |
| 10 min | 16.8% |
| 20 min | 13.7% |
| 30 min | 10.5% |
| 40 min | 7.3% |
| 50 min | 4.2% |
| 60 min | 1.0% |

**The exact formula:**
```
if (timeSinceLaunch < 3600 seconds):
    fee = 2000 - (timeSinceLaunch x (2000 - 100) / 3600)
else:
    fee = 100
```

Fee is in basis points (2000 = 20%, 100 = 1%). Linear interpolation between 20% and 1%.

Launch fees go to the same Distributor as normal swap fees. The first hour of trading, when fees are highest, generates the largest single batch of rewards for presale holders and farm stakers.

## Anti-Sniper Protection

The elevated fee window makes sniping costly. Bots that buy at launch and dump on retail need to overcome the round-trip fee before they profit.

**Round-trip cost at launch (buy + sell at time 0):**
- Buy: 20% fee on token output (you receive 80% of expected tokens)
- Sell immediately: 20% fee on ETH output (you receive 80% of expected ETH)
- Combined: you keep ~64% of your capital. Need a ~56% price increase just to break even.

**Round-trip at 30 minutes (10.5% fee):**
- Combined retention: ~80%. Break-even requires ~25% price increase.

**Round-trip at 50 minutes (4.2% fee):**
- Combined retention: ~92%. Break-even requires ~9% price increase.

The design doesn't promise zero sniping. It makes the first-hour window expensive enough that casual bots can't profit from small price movements.

## Where Swap Fees Go

All swap fees flow to the Distributor, which routes them based on claim progress:

**ETH from swap fees:**

| Recipient | At Launch | After 50% Claimed | After 100% Claimed |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Ponzu protocol | 40% | 40% | 40% |
| Presale holders | 20% | 10% | 0% |
| Farm stakers | 20% | 30% | 40% |
| Project treasury | 15% | 15% | 15% |
| Kioke holders | 5% | 5% | 5% |

**Worked example (first-hour fees):**

$100,000 in buy volume during the first hour, average fee ~10.5%:
- Total fees collected: ~$10,500
- Ponzu protocol: $4,200
- Presale holders: $2,100 (if 0% claimed) or $1,050 (if 50% claimed)
- Farm stakers: $2,100 (if 0% claimed) or $3,150 (if 50% claimed)
- Project treasury: $1,575
- Kioke holders: $525

These are the largest fee payouts any project will generate. First-hour volume is typically the highest, and the fee rate is 10-20x normal.

## Presale Purchase Fee (4%)

Separate from swap fees. Every presale purchase has a 4% fee on ETH input, distributed immediately:

| Recipient | Share |
|-----------|-------|
| Creator (project deployer) | 1% |
| Ponzu protocol | 1% |
| Kioke holders | 1% |
| Order referrer | 1% |

This fee is deducted from your ETH before it enters the presale pool. On a 1 ETH purchase, 0.04 ETH goes to fees and 0.96 ETH goes toward your token allocation.

**Interaction with refunds:** If you refund, you get 90% of the ETH in the presale pool for your position. The 4% fee was already distributed at purchase time and is not recoverable. On a 1 ETH purchase followed by a refund: 0.04 ETH in fees, 0.96 ETH entered the pool, 90% of your proportional share (~0.864 ETH) comes back. Total round-trip cost: ~0.136 ETH (13.6%).

## Refund Penalty

Before launch, buyers can refund with a 10% token penalty:
- 10% of your token allocation stays in the presale pool
- The remaining tokens are removed from your allocation
- 90% of your proportional ETH is returned
- The penalty tokens are redistributed pro-rata to all remaining presale holders

This is a token penalty, not an ETH penalty. You lose 10% of your tokens, and the ETH returned is proportional to your remaining allocation.

## Farm Early Exit Penalty

Unstaking LP from the farm before 7 days incurs a linear penalty. Full details on the [Non-Inflationary Farming](/docs/non-inflationary-farming) page.

100% penalty at day 0, linearly decreasing to 0% at day 7. Penalized LP splits 50/50 between remaining farm stakers and protocol-owned liquidity.

## Complete Fee Summary

| Fee | Amount | When | Destination | Refundable? |
|-----|--------|------|-------------|-------------|
| Presale purchase | 4% of ETH | Each presale buy | Creator, protocol, Kioke, referrer (1% each) | No |
| Swap (launch window) | 20% decaying to 1% | First hour after launch | Distributor | N/A |
| Swap (normal) | 1% of output | Every trade after first hour | Distributor | N/A |
| Refund penalty | 10% of tokens | Before launch, on refund | Remaining presale holders | N/A |
| Farm early exit | Up to 100% of LP | First 7 days of farm stake | 50% stakers, 50% protocol | N/A |

Every fee is deterministic, on-chain, and calculable before you commit. No hidden costs. No variable admin fees. No fee changes post-deployment.

## On-Chain Verification

| What to Check | How |
|---------------|-----|
| Current swap fee rate | Read `launchTime` from the pair contract, calculate elapsed time, apply the formula above |
| Fee recipient | The Distributor address is set at pair creation and visible in the pair contract |
| Fee on a specific trade | Use the router's `getAmountsOut()` function. The returned amount already accounts for the current fee. |
| Launch timestamp | Call `launchTime()` on the pair contract |

The fee formula is hardcoded in the pair contract bytecode. No admin can change the 1% normal fee, the 20% launch fee, or the 60-minute decay window.
